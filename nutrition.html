Understanding Macronutrients

Macronutrient Calories per gram Primary Role Good Sources
Protein 4 kcal/g Muscle repair & growth, satiety Chicken, eggs, fish, beef, Greek yogurt, legumes
Carbohydrates 4 kcal/g Primary energy source, brain fuel Rice, oats, potatoes, fruit, bread, pasta
Fats 9 kcal/g Hormones, joint health, fat-soluble vitamins Olive oil, avocado, nuts, eggs, fatty fish

Setting Your Calorie Target

Goal Calorie Adjustment Expected Outcome
Lose Fat 300-500 kcal below maintenance 0.3-0.5 kg fat loss per week
Maintain Weight At maintenance calories Body recomposition (if training hard)
Build Muscle 200-400 kcal above maintenance Lean muscle gain with minimal fat

How to find your maintenance calories: Multiply your body weight in kg by 33 for a sedentary lifestyle, 38 for moderate activity, or 42 for high activity levels.

Protein: Your Most Important Macro

Protein is the building block of muscle. Without adequate protein, no amount of training will produce the results you're looking for. Aim for 1.6-2.2 grams of protein per kilogram of bodyweight per day.

Spread your protein intake across 3-5 meals for optimal muscle protein synthesis throughout the day.

High Protein Meal Ideas

  • Chicken breast with rice and vegetables
  • Greek yogurt with oats and berries
  • Eggs and whole grain toast
  • Tuna salad with whole grain crackers
  • Lentil soup with whole grain bread
  • Beef stir-fry with noodles

Meal Timing

Pre-Workout

Eat a balanced meal 1.5-2 hours before training. Include carbohydrates for energy and moderate protein. Avoid very high fat or fibre meals before intense sessions.

Post-Workout

Eat within 1-2 hours after training. Prioritize protein (30-40g) and carbohydrates to replenish glycogen and kickstart muscle recovery.

Before Bed

A slow-digesting protein source like cottage cheese or casein before sleep can support overnight muscle recovery and reduce muscle breakdown.

Hydration

  • Aim for at least 2-3 litres of water per day.
  • Add an extra 500-750 ml for every hour of exercise.
  • If your urine is pale yellow, you're well hydrated. Dark yellow means drink more.
  • Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) matter too — especially if you sweat heavily.
